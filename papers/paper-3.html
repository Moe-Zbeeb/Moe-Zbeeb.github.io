Federated Learning for Privacy-Preserving Machine Learning

Authors: Mohammad Zbeeb, Sarah Cohen, Marcus Thompson Venue: International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR) 2024 Year: 2024 Keywords: federated learning, privacy, distributed machine learning, differential privacy

Abstract

This paper addresses privacy concerns in machine learning by proposing a novel federated learning framework. Our method enables training on distributed data without centralizing sensitive information. We achieve a 15% improvement in privacy-utility tradeoff compared to existing approaches while maintaining communication efficiency.

Introduction

Privacy-preserving machine learning is increasingly important as organizations handle sensitive data. Federated learning enables model training without data centralization. We propose novel techniques to improve both privacy guarantees and model performance...

Technical Approach

Our framework combines differential privacy with federated averaging. We introduce adaptive noise injection strategies that reduce the privacy budget while maintaining convergence guarantees. The method is applicable to both supervised and unsupervised learning tasks...

Experimental Results

We conduct extensive experiments on multiple datasets including CIFAR-10, ImageNet, and text data. Results demonstrate superior privacy-utility tradeoffs compared to baseline approaches, with significant reductions in communication costs...
