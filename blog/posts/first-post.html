On Expressivity and Simplicity in Model Design

2025-01-01

Simple baselines often provide a surprisingly strong reference point for more involved architectures. The key is that inductive bias aligns with the data distribution and objective. As a rule of thumb, favor hypotheses that preserve interpretability.

Two practical reminders: (1) compare at equal training budgets; (2) ablate one variable at a time. These controls prevent confounding explanations and keep iteration loops tight.

When a more complex model wins, scrutinize the where and why: optimization stability, data regime, or regularization strength.
