In Arabic, حلا (Hala) conveys sweetness and beauty - qualities long associated with the language itself. In this spirit, we present Hala, our family of Arabic-centric instruction and translation models.

Introduction

Large language models have revolutionized natural language processing, but most advances have been concentrated on English. While multilingual models exist, they often struggle with the linguistic nuances, cultural context, and dialectal variations of Arabic - a language spoken by over 400 million people worldwide.

Today, we're excited to introduce Hala, a family of Arabic-centric instruction and translation models that addresses this gap. Rather than treating Arabic as just another language in a multilingual system, Hala puts Arabic at the center, optimizing specifically for the depth and quality of Arabic language understanding.

Our Approach: Language-Centric Modeling

Instead of building another general multilingual model, we adopted a language-centric approach - one that prioritizes depth of capability in Arabic while still benefiting from cross-lingual transfer when appropriate.

Cross-lingual translation and fine-tuning pipeline for building Hala models. The process includes teacher compression, bilingual supervision creation, and model merging.

Key Innovations

  1. Efficient Translation Infrastructure: FP8 compression achieving ~2� speedup with no quality loss
  2. Million-Scale Bilingual Supervision: High-fidelity Arabic instruction data from quality English sources
  3. Lightweight Translator Training: Specialized Arabic�English translator for instruction-style inputs
  4. Smart Model Merging: Spherical linear interpolation to balance specialization and general capability

Quick Start

Python - Getting Started with Hala
from transformers import AutoModelForCausalLM, AutoTokenizer

# Load Hala-1.2B model
model = AutoModelForCausalLM.from_pretrained("hammh0a/Hala-1.2B")
tokenizer = AutoTokenizer.from_pretrained("hammh0a/Hala-1.2B")

# Arabic instruction example
prompt = "اشرح لي مفهوم الذكاء الاصطناعي بطريقة بسيطة"
inputs = tokenizer(prompt, return_tensors="pt")
outputs = model.generate(**inputs, max_length=200)
response = tokenizer.decode(outputs[0], skip_special_tokens=True)
print(response)
