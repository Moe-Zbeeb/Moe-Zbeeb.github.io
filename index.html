Research

My research focuses on machine learning, with particular emphasis on model merging, reasoning models, and Arabic natural language processing.

I am currently working on developing techniques to transfer reasoning capabilities to base language models without relying on reinforcement learning, test-time scaling, or chain-of-thought fine-tuning. Through model merging approaches, I have reduced computation from 4-5 hours on GPUs to streamlined 30-second pipelines, making advanced reasoning capabilities more accessible.

I am also building benchmarks for Arabic translation using large language models, including dataset creation and evaluation frameworks. As part of this work, I am leading the release of MergeSuite, a family of models and datasets designed to standardize model merging research and provide the community with accessible tools for benchmarking and integration.

Additionally, I work on practical applications of machine learning at the edge, developing defensive models against network attacks, efficient language models for resource-constrained environments, and state-of-the-art phishing detection systems.
